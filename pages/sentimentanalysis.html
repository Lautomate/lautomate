Sentiment Analysis

Level: Intermediate

Tools and Techniques: Python, Power BI, and DAX

Topic: Sentiment

NOTE

The dataset used in this project was created by me and replicates actual sentiment analysis in the real world. New data is updated regularly up to the end of the year.

GOAL

The goal of this project is to extract emotions from text, whether positive, negative, or neutral, using Python and visualize it in Power BI. This makes it easy for businesses to understand user perceptions of their products.

INTRODUCTION

Texts provide a rich source of qualitative information in data analysis, revealing hidden insights such as opinions, emotions, and sentiments. By creating a sentiment analysis, businesses can improve products, enhance brand reputation, and strengthen customer relations.

Through the proper use of tools like VADER Sentiment and Power BI to visualize the results, I delivered a full-scale project that positions businesses to better understand how they approach their customers based on customer sentiments.

PROCESS

The first step I took was to scan the dataset. I found that not all text was written in English; it included different languages from various users. The VADER Sentiment tool only accepts English text, and manually translating non-English text was not feasible as it counters the motive of automation. Therefore, I used a Python library called googleTrans to detect and translate non-English text into English. Given the dataset's size, I utilized asynchronous methods to handle the large volume of translations, along with filtering techniques to translate only non-English texts.

Some texts involved irony, sarcasm, or dialects that are difficult to translate into English, which posed challenges for the tool. To address these elements, I created a custom sentiment extension using Python lists. I then tested the results to ensure that the texts were accurately tagged as positive, negative, or neutral.

Finally, I loaded the data to perform visualization and add necessary DAX elements in order to showcase the results.

LIMITATION

Some idiomatic expressions, sarcasm, and cultural references may not be accurately captured by the sentiment analysis, even with the custom sentiment extension I created, as the growing volume of data makes it challenging to handle and may lead to misclassification of sentiments.

RESULT

The sentiment analysis revealed valuable insights into customer perceptions, providing a clear breakdown of positive, negative, and neutral sentiments. Visualizations in Power BI highlighted trends and patterns, allowing stakeholders to identify areas for improvement and better understand user feedback. Overall, the project successfully demonstrated the potential of sentiment analysis in enhancing business strategies and customer relations.
